About Me

Hi there! My name is Shanice, and I am a Junior Full Stack Developer.

My very first experience with coding was when I was 13, when Friendster became popular in the Philippines. It's like Facebook but users can customize the look of their pages using HTML and CSS. I found that really fun so I decided to take it more seriously by taking a computers course as an elective during my high school there. During my final year in 2008, I got accepted to one of Philippine's top universities, University of Santo Tomas, for Information Technology. But I wasn't able to enroll as my family was moving to Canada.

When we moved to Canada, I developed an interest in the environment and renewable energy, so I decided to pursue an Electrical Engineering degree. That's where I learned my very first programming language, C++. During my time in university, I also further practiced my programming skills with C, C#, MIPS Assembly, VHDL. I also joined the university's Solar Car team where I get to play a little bit with the Arduino. For the last year of our undergrad, I was able to make our Capstone team succeed as Project Manager. We successfully built a wearable device that senses the user's muscle electric activity and increase their grip strength, also with an Arduino.

Now, I have shifted my interest back to IT, specifically Software/Web Development. I am finding everything that I am doing fun and engaging, and I am learning new things each day!
